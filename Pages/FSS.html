Filipino Style Spaghetti
  INGREDIENTS

  • 1 lb. spaghetti noodles
  • 1 tbsp. oil
  • 4 Filipino-style hotdogs, sliced diagonally
  • 3 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 bell pepper, seeded, cored and chopped
  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • Â½ cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup banana ketchup (preferably sweet and spicy)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tsp. sugar
  • Â½ cup quick-melt cheese, shredded

 		  PROCEDURE
  1. Cook the spaghetti noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and bell pepper, cook for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add garlic and bell pepper, cook for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  4. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking into small pieces. Drain any excess fat.
  5. Add tomato sauce, tomato paste, banana ketchup, and beef broth. Stir well and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the sauce becomes too thick, add water in Â¼ cup increments.
  7. During the last 10 minutes of simmering, add the sliced hotdogs and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Toss cooked spaghetti with the sauce, then top with shredded cheese and serve.
Filipino-style spaghetti is a unique and delicious take on the classic Italian dish,
incorporating sweet and savory flavors and unexpected ingredients like hotdogs.
