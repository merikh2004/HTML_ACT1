Leche Flan
  INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 12 egg yolks
  • 1 can (387ml) condensed milk
  • 1 can (354ml) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract















 		  PROCEDURE
  1. Combine sugar and water in a saucepan over medium heat. Swirl the pan occasionally to dissolve the sugar, but avoid stirring directly.
  2. Cook until the mixture turns golden brown and begins to caramelize. Watch closely to avoid burning.
  3. Immediately pour the hot caramel into your chosen mold or baking dish, tilting it to coat the bottom evenly. Set aside to cool and harden.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks until well combined.
  5. Slowly whisk in the condensed milk and evaporated milk until smooth.
  6. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  7. To ensure a silky smooth texture, strain the custard mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. This step removes any stray egg whites or lumps.
  8. Preheat your oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C).Place the mold or dish containing the hardened caramel in a larger baking dish.
  9. Carefully pour the custard mixture into the mold, ensuring it doesn't mix with the caramel.Cover the mold tightly with aluminum foil.
  10. Pour hot water into the larger baking dish, reaching about halfway up the sides of the mold (bain-marie method).
  11. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  12. Remove the flan from the oven and let it cool completely in the water bath. Once cool, carefully refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight for best results.
  13. Run a knife along the edges of the flan to loosen it from the mold. Invert the flan onto a serving plate, revealing the beautiful caramel topping.
  14. Enjoy chilled!
Leche Flan is a baked custard dessert with a layer of caramel on top. To make flan, you first caramelize sugar,
pour it into a round mold or individual baking cups, and then top it with custard made from eggs, milk or
other dairy, sugar and flavorings
